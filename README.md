# Project Title

Pumped Thermal Energy Storage Designer and Simulator

## Description

This work represents public Pumped thermal energy storage design and simulation web application at
https://anttisaarenheimo.github.io/ and the related research. The browser application designs and simulates
an exceptionally large seasonal thermal battery of electric power built into an open pit filled with crushed
stone. Axial turbomachines of storage implement a two-way closed Brayton cycle to charge and discharge
electricity as extreme heat and cryogenic cold to and from crushed stone made from waste rock. This work also
presents the other works, and their thermodynamic formulas and algorithms embedded in the software. We
inspect the technical details of the concept by representing the other works on geology, mineralogy, rock
engineering, fluid flow mechanics, metallurgy, thermodynamics, power engineering, turbomachinery and
mean-line loss models of axial compressors and turbines. The web page designs energy storage, its optimized
axial turbomachines, heat exchangers, calculates the efficiency predictions for the turbomachines and closed
two-way Brayton cycle, and finally simulates the digital storage and its profitability with historical European
power market data.

The default parameters of the web application produce 946 GWh/2403 MW energy storage. Its round-trip
efficiency predicted by the best mean-line loss models and fluid dynamics formulas is 90.4%. Exceptionally high
round-trip efficiency is explained by optimized subsonic axial turbomachines, very low inlet temperature of
cryogenic compressor and high turbine inlet temperature. The axial turbomachines are quite compact
compared to the huge synchronous motor-generators but together they have tremendous inertia stabilizing
the grid frequency. Energy density of storage is only about 10% of lithium-ion battery, but the capacity of a 45
mt storage is three times the current worldwide production of lithium-ion batteries1.

The web page uses unit cost parameters based on public information to predict the cost of energy storage. The
default values predict 903 million euros as total cost of energy storage, the plant, and all machinery. The capex
costs of energy storage, 0.92 €/kWh and 376 €/kW, are extremely low compared to any other grid scale battery
storage solution2. Respectively the cost of additional storage capacity is 0.36 €/kWh and the cost of additional
power 234 €/kW. These very competitive costs are explained by large scale of the plant, high round-trip
efficiency, very low cost of crushed stone processing and simplicity of synchronous standalone axial
turbomachines. Simulation of digital plant in the power market of Germany predicts 42% ROI for year 2025
when operating costs are €10m and electricity price elasticity is 3%/500 MW. Low-cost seasonal energy storage
enables the replacement of existing coal, oil and natural gas power plants with more competitive solar and
wind power. Fossil fuel electricity generation accounts for 40% of global energy-related carbon dioxide
emissions.
